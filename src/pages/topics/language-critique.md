## Language Usage & Critique (EN • DE • IT • GR • BG)

Notes and critiques on specific language usage, common errors, and nuances across languages.

| Concept | EN | DE | IT | GR | BG | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Critiques & Nuances** |
| Incorrect use of "eben" | You can't say "kann oder eben nicht kann" | **kann oder eben nicht kann** | non si può dire "kann oder eben nicht kann" | δεν μπορείς να πεις "kann oder eben nicht kann" | не може да се каже "kann oder eben nicht kann" | The German particle "eben" is not used this way to mean "just" or "simply". |
| "Al contrario" vs. "inversamente" | "on the contrary" | Al contrario | **Al contrario** vs. **inversamente** | σε αντίθεση | напротив | In Italian, "Al contrario" is common. "inversamente" is quite rare. |
| "Now it turns out that..." | Now it turns out that... | Jetzt stellt sich heraus, dass... | **Adesso salta fuori che...** | Τώρα αποδεικνύεται ότι... | Сега се оказва, че... | A very native-sounding Italian phrase, literally "it jumps out". |
| "Bravo, nothing to add" | Bravo, nothing to add | Bravo, nichts hinzuzufügen | **Bravo bravo, nient'altro da aggiungere** | Μπράβο, τίποτα να προσθέσω | Браво, няма какво да се добави | A common Italian way to express strong agreement and praise. |
| "very little time" | very little time | sehr wenig Zeit | **pochissimo tempo** (not "Tanto poco tempo") | πολύ λίγος χρόνος | много малко време | "Tanto poco tempo" is incorrect in Italian. |
| Attaching pronouns to infinitives | to talk about it | darüber reden | **parlarne** (not "Per ne parlare") | να μιλήσω γι' αυτό | да говоря за това | In Italian, when a pronoun like "ne" is used with an infinitive verb, it attaches to the end. |
| Italian `ci` & `ne` particles | - | - | **ci** & **ne** | - | - | `ci` often refers to a location ("there") or is part of pronominal verbs (metterci, volerci). `ne` often refers to a quantity ("of it/them") or a topic ("about it"). |
| "chances are" | chances are | wahrscheinlich | **è probabile che** | οι πιθανότητες είναι | има вероятност | Italian has no identical expression, but "è probabile che" is a good equivalent. |
| "in front of" | in front of | vor | **davanti a** / **di fronte a** | μπροστά από | пред | In Italian, "di fronte a" is more literal for physical position (e.g., "in front of the church"). "davanti a" is more general. |
| Subtitles | subtitles | Untertitel | **sottotitoli** | υπότιτλοι | субтитри | In Italian, it's "sottotitoli", using "sotto-" (under) instead of "sub-". |
| "to a great extent" spelling | to a great extent | in hohem Maße | in larga misura | σε μεγάλο βαθμό | до голяма степен | Correct spelling is "extent", not "extend". |
